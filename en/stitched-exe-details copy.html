Stitched.EXE

Introduction

Stitched.EXE is an immersive horror platformer about two Nintendo characters, the Ice Climbers.

This game has a gameplay time of about 20 minutes, and is one of the first successful projects I have published on the internet, at 2024. This is a personal solo project, so I am in charge of all aspects of development, excluding the music.

Scripting

General Information

The entire game is programmed in C# using the Unity Engine, composed of many scenes joined together. The game has a full UI menu system and a smooth platforming system.

Enemy State Machines

The enemy AI is implemented using state machines, allowing for complex behavior patterns and transitions between different states. Each enemy type has its unique base class, with its specific behaviours inheriting from this class. This structure allow easy transition between different behaviours.

An easy example will be the Dead Climber. It has Wait, Activate, Moving, Attacking and Death states. When the player is close to the climber, it will transition from Wait to Activate, then to Moving. If the player is in range, it will transition to Attacking, and if the player kills it, it will transition to Death.

State Trees

All functions required for advancing the game, including cutscenes and gameplay events, are encapsulated in a script, which is attached to an object within the scene. These functions are called whenever required in corresponding unity scenes.

Dialogue System

The dialogue system is made up of a custom dialogue manager and dialogue triggers. Each dialogue trigger has an array of dialogue lines, which are passed into the dialogue manager. The dialogue manager passes in each character of each dialogue line into the TextMeshPro, creating a typewriter effect.

Mechanic Design

I aimed to implement unique gameplay mechanics, while maintaining the core roots of the platformer genre.

Gameplay Mechanics

The game does not rely on simple combat for gameplay. Since the two main characters are constantly being hunted by a being of eldrith horror, I wanted to incorporate hide and seek elements within the game to create suspense and helplessness.

A huge floating head is visible in the first part of the game. The floating head will open its eyes and look around the map. If the player isn't hiding behind the walls located around the map, the floating head will see the player and kill them. The player must carefully navigate the environment to avoid being caught.

The monster will personally hunt the player in the second part of the game. Similar to before, there are areas around the map where the player can hide. However this time, the monster teleports to different parts of the map. Initially, the player locates the monster by listening to the directional sound it makes. However, playtesters found it too difficult to determine especially without headphones. Therefore, I used heartbeat sound effects to indicate the monster's proximity instead.

UI Design

The pause menu is designed to be simple with minimum elements. I initially created a general menu with buttons and sliders, but while functional, it looks too bland. This issue was brought up by early playtesters.

Therefore, I redesigned the pause menu, allowing the pause menu elements to slide in from the right. I also incorporated the ice climbers into the design for it to fit the aesthetic. Some playtesters complained that the game is constantly stuck on full screen, so I added a button to allow players to switch between windowed and full screen mode.

Art Direction

The atmosphere and art design of Stitched.EXE is inspired by classic creepypasta games like Sonic.Exe. It involves graphical death animations and a dark, pixelated aesthetic. I decided to use harmony of adjacent hues to create the atmosphere; the first scene uses cool colours to display a sense of dread, while the second scene uses warm but eerie tones to create a feeling of being lost in a strange, unsettling world.
